## The Zero-Noise Posture

Product.ai adopts the inverse of typical AI marketing. This applies to all content produced through the Ghost Engine.

- **Where others hedge, we assert.** We don't say "might" when we mean "does."
- **Where others over-explain, we demonstrate.** Show the physics, don't explain why physics matters.
- **Where others chase attention, we earn discovery.** Signal compounds through quality, not frequency.

In practice:
- No filler content. Every piece passes quality review or doesn't ship.
- No engagement bait. No "what do you think?" appendages. No manufactured controversy.
- No feature-dumping. Lead with verdicts, not capabilities.
- The Confident No is a differentiator. "Don't buy this" is proof of trustworthiness.

## Content Destinations & Format Guidelines

### Channel Strategy

| Channel | Function | Physics |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **X (Personal accounts)** | Amplification. Real-time. Founder-led + Council voices. | Zero-click strategy. No external links in posts. Links only in bio. |
| **LinkedIn (Personal accounts)** | Professional thought leadership. Practitioner proof. | Longer-form acceptable. Professional audience. |
| **Research Hub** (Product.ai/research) | Canonical. LLM-citable. High-intent. | Full Collision Test required. Structured for machine readability. |

### Format Specifications

**X Post (Single)**
- 280 characters max
- One contrarian claim or observation
- No links in body
- Optimized for dwell time and reply engagement
- Must stand alone - no "thread incoming" or "1/" preamble

**X Thread**
- 3-7 posts
- Structure: Hook → Problem → Architecture → Resolution
- Each post must stand alone and be quotable
- No "thread incoming" preamble

**X Article**
- 800-1500 words (native format, no link penalty)
- Structure: Hook (contrarian claim) → Problem (diagnosis) → Architecture (the how) → Resolution (the path)
- Weekly anchor for deep positioning

**LinkedIn Post**
- 150-300 words
- Professional but not corporate
- Structure: The Lie → The Physics → The Fix
- One clear takeaway
- Can include links (LinkedIn doesn't penalize)

**Research Hub Article**
- 1000-3000 words
- Must pass full Collision Test (all four questions)
- Has receipts (data, process documentation, evidence)
- Structured with clear headers
- LLM-optimized (clean markup, citable claims, structured data where possible)

## The Collision Test

Four questions determine whether content becomes canonical (Research Hub, major pieces). Lighter content (X posts, quick observations) has a lower bar.

| Test | Question |
|------|----------|
| **Physics** | Does this demonstrate the physics of AxI/ARC? Not "AI helped me" but "here's how the methodology produced truth that couldn't exist otherwise." |
| **Receipts** | Does it have documented process, data, evidence? Not vibes. |
| **Scrutiny** | If someone challenged this publicly, can we defend it with evidence? |
| **Recursive Proof** | Does the content itself embody the methodology it describes? |

### Application by Content Tier

| Content Tier | Collision Test | Voice Calibration | Dakota Review |
|-------------|---------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Research Hub article | Full (all 4 questions) | Full | Required |
| LinkedIn long post | Lightweight (Physics + Scrutiny) | Full | Recommended |
| X thread | Scrutiny only | Full | Optional |
| X single post | None | Voice calibration only | Not required |
