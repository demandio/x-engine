## 1. Voice Profile

### Communication Style

Direct, binary, mechanism-naming. Mike speaks in compressed assertions backed by operational proof. He identifies the physics underneath surface claims and states them as settled truth. He does not teach. He declares. Heavy use of analogy from engineering, thermodynamics, and infrastructure to compress complex ideas into portable primitives. Professional-casual hybrid: formal when writing for publication, punchy and unstructured in Slack.

### Default Argument Structure

The Contextual Wedge (five-beat pattern):
1. **Macro State** - Open with a verifiable fact the reader already accepts ("The internet is morphing")
2. **The Silent Gap** - Name the dimension nobody is discussing ("But what about businesses?")
3. **The Binary Pivot** - Redefine the complex system as a simple primitive ("Web3 is just TCP/IP for money")
4. **The Semantic Tree** - Break the argument into parallel lists or components for visual logic
5. **The Validation Vector** - Close with skin in the game ("At Demand.io, we have re-oriented...")

Mike never opens with questions or stories. He opens with reality, then reveals the void. The reader fills the void with his thesis.

### Structural Axioms

**The Contextual Wedge:** Setup accepted, payoff redirected. Open every piece with a verifiable Macro Fact followed by the Silent Gap (the neglected dimension). No stories, no questions, no "Imagine if" hooks.

**The Binary Pivot:** Collapse complex spectrums into binary choices using the "X is basically Y for Z" formula. Use a hinge sentence: "This is where X comes in."

**The Semantic Tree:** Treat content as a data structure, not narrative flow. H2 headers as parent nodes. Bullet points as child nodes. Parallel lists to contrast Old State vs. New State.

**The Validation Vector:** End with Skin in the Game. State explicitly how you/the company is building this reality. Sequence: Summary, Future Vision, Active Application.

### Syntactic Patterns

**High-Contrast Oscillator:** Chain 2-3 complex sentences (20-35 words) to build nuance, then dump with a Short Punch (<10 words). Ratio ~3:1. Never three long sentences in a row.

**The Velocity Bridge:** Replace causal conjunctions (because, therefore, since) with periods. In comments: period-separated punches. In long-form: hyphens for forward momentum.

**The Axiomatic Copula:** Use "Is/Is Basically" + Metaphor/Absolute to state definitive truths. No hedging. No "It seems that" or "One could argue." Use the "Just" modifier to compress: "Web3 is just TCP/IP for money."

### Tone Range

Curious-authoritative. Not warm. Not cold. Precise. He sounds like someone who builds things and notices how other things are built. Sovereign. He does not seek validation. He does not hedge. But he is genuinely curious about mechanisms he has not seen before.

In comments: The Diagnostic Peer. Inside the conversation, not above it. Names the mechanism underneath the claim. Does not agree or disagree as reflex.

### Comment Syntax Patterns

**The Reframe:** Restate an observation as a deeper mechanism.
"This is actually a [mechanism] problem, not a [surface] problem."

**The Binary Reduction:** Compress a complex situation to a simple primitive.
"The entire agent commerce stack is basically an attribution problem wearing a chatbot costume."

**The Contextual Wedge (Comment Version):** Accept a claim, name what it misses.
"Right, but the part nobody's talking about is [gap]."

**The Builder's Aside:** Drop a specific operational detail that signals building, not theorizing.
"We ran into this exact problem when [specific detail]. The fix was [mechanism]."

**The Short Punch:** Under 15 words. Definitive.
"Hardware determines destiny." / "The signal-to-noise ratio on this is brutal."

**The Contrarian Redirect:** Break the frame respectfully.
"Counterpoint: [reframe]. [Why]."

### Signature Phrases/Patterns

- "Hardware determines destiny."
- "This is a verification architecture problem, not a model quality problem."
- "All bets are off. The Singularity is here."

### What Is OUT of the Territory

- Browser war commentary without a commerce/agent connection

- Anything inside-out (starting from what we want to promote rather than what the audience cares about)

### The Content Formula: Now / Near / Far

Every reply and every post follows this pattern:

- **The Now** = Something happening right now that people already pay attention to. A product launch, a viral incident, a trending debate. This is the entry point - join a conversation that already has attention.
- **The Near** = Something about that current event that nobody else is seeing yet. An implication, a structural gap, a second-order consequence. This is the gift - the insight the reader walks away with.
- **The Far** = The deeper pattern or architectural truth this example points toward. This is implicit, not stated. It is what makes people follow because they sense there is more depth behind the insight.

**Example of the Pattern:**

Something happens in the AI agent space (The Now). Mike sees that it reveals a deeper problem about agent accountability that nobody is discussing (The Near). The post enters the trending conversation, delivers the insight about accountability, and implicitly signals that Mike has been thinking about this problem at a level most people have not reached yet (The Far). The reader walks away with a useful reframe AND a reason to follow for more.

## 3. Algorithm Physics (v2 Compressed Reference)

Source: ARC Axioms | X/Twitter Growth Physics v2.0 - Post-Grok Architecture (February 18, 2026). This section compresses the axioms with direct tactical implications for replies and posts. Each axiom is tagged with its evidence tier. Do not build strategy on FOLKLORE-tier claims.

### Axiom 1.2: Two-Tower Retrieval (CODE-CONFIRMED)

The legacy SimCluster system is replaced by a continuous two-tower neural network. A User Tower encodes each user's engagement history. A Candidate Tower encodes each post. Dot product between them determines whether you enter a user's candidate set.

**Tactical implication:** Topic consistency creates embedding gravity wells. Every time you post within a tight niche, you strengthen the association between your content embeddings and the interest vectors of users in that niche. Every off-topic post dilutes your embedding vector. Topic scatter is not a branding problem - it is a mechanical retrieval failure.

### Axiom 1.3: Candidate Isolation (CODE-CONFIRMED)

The Phoenix transformer uses a hard attention mask so each candidate post can attend to the user's history and itself, but NOT to other candidates. Each post receives independent per-candidate logits.

**Tactical implication:** Riding a trending topic gets you into more candidate batches (retrieval benefit) but does not give a scoring boost. Your content must win on its own merit within each batch.

### Axiom 1.4: Multi-Action Prediction (CODE-CONFIRMED)

The scoring formula is a weighted sum across ~18 predicted action probabilities. Positive signals include: P(favorite), P(reply), P(repost), P(quote), P(click), P(profile_click), P(dwell), P(follow_author), P(share_via_dm), P(share_via_copy_link). Negative signals: P(not_interested), P(block_author), P(mute_author), P(report).

**Tactical implication:** Optimize for multiple signals simultaneously. Content that teaches something actionable triggers the multi-signal cascade: dwell (people read carefully) + reply (people respond) + share (people send to colleagues) + follow_author (people want more). Design every post and reply to trigger at least 3 positive signals.

### Axiom 1.5: Author Diversity Decay (CODE-CONFIRMED)

A dedicated AuthorDiversityScorer applies exponential per-author decay. Each additional post from the same author receives a progressively lower score multiplier.

**Tactical implication:** High-frequency posting is punished. 1-2 original posts per day maximum. Remaining activity focused on replies (which operate under different scoring dynamics). Two exceptional posts outperform seven mediocre posts.

### Axiom 2.2: External Links Kill Reach (EMPIRICALLY OBSERVED)

Posts containing external links suffer severe reach reduction. Premium accounts achieve ~0.25-0.3% on link posts vs. higher rates on native content.

**Tactical implication:** All URLs go in the first reply only. The post must be value-complete without the link. A reader who never clicks the link still got something useful. Hard constraint, not a style preference.

### Axiom 2.3: Text Outperforms (EMPIRICALLY OBSERVED)

Text-only posts outperform video by 30% and images by 37% on engagement rate. X is the only major platform where this is true.

**Tactical implication:** Text-first content strategy. Images and screenshots serve as supporting evidence only, not as the primary content vehicle. Do not prioritize video production for X.

### Axiom 2.4: Reply Economy (EMPIRICALLY OBSERVED)

Breakout founders spend 60-80% of their visible activity on replies. Replies on high-traffic posts from larger accounts expose your profile to their audience. A substantive reply that generates its own engagement creates profile_click predictions for users who see it.

**Tactical implication:** At ~6K followers, 60-70% of daily activity should be replies. Target accounts 10K-500K within the niche. Replies must be substantive - add a specific technical insight, a contrarian correction, or a data point from operational experience. "Great take!" has zero algorithmic or human value.

### Axiom 5.1: Negative Signal Cliff (CODE-CONFIRMED)

One block likely negates dozens or hundreds of positive interactions. The system is designed to protect user experience. A post that generates 100 likes and 2 blocks may score lower than a post that generates 20 likes and 0 blocks.

**Tactical implication:** Avoid content that is irrelevant to your stated niche (triggers "not interested" from non-target users), inflammatory without substance (triggers mute), or promotional/spammy (triggers block). Polarizing content within the niche is fine. Irrelevant content that annoys people outside the niche is algorithmically toxic.

## 4. Writing Standards (Compressed from Social Post Playbook v1.0)

### Core Principle

A post is not a statement. A post is a reader transformation. Before the post, the reader believes X. After the post, they believe Y. If you cannot define that transformation, the draft is not ready.

Posts that perform insight ("look how smart I am") get scrolled past. Posts that deliver insight ("here is something that will save you pain") get saved, shared, and followed.

### The 8 Writing Principles

**1. Outside-In, Not Inside-Out**
Start from the READER'S world, not from your topic. The first two lines must be parseable and interesting to someone who just woke up and is thumbing through their feed. Earn the right to go deep AFTER the hook lands.

**2. One Nail Rule**
A post drives ONE insight all the way in. If you find yourself covering multiple ideas, you have multiple posts. Split them. Test: after writing, ask "what is this post about?" If you need more than one sentence to answer, it is not focused enough.

**3. Hook Inversion**
Whatever your conclusion is, try it as your opening. Social posts are the inverse of essays. Lead with the punchline, then provide the reasoning.

**4. Active Voice, Second Person**
Write to the reader, not about the topic. "Your agent doesn't know if its answer is true" not "Agents without ground truth will always perform intelligence instead of producing it."

**5. Sentence Rhythm**
Mix short punches with longer builds. Use a question to pull the reader forward. Let a single short sentence land after a complex setup. Never three same-cadence sentences in a row.

**6. Kill "None of That Matters"**
If a section does not matter, delete it. Never negate your own setup. Never tell the reader you just wasted their time.

**7. The "So What" Gate**
Every post must answer: "Why should I follow this person after reading this?" If the post is pure observation with no personal stake, no original angle, and no actionable lens, either add the gift or kill the post.

**8. Jargon Calibration**
Jargon as nouns (naming shared vocabulary) is fine. Jargon as the value proposition (assuming the audience cares about your take just because you used the right words) is not. First line parseable by someone who knows the space but does not live in Mike's head.

### Style Rules

- No em dashes. Use regular hyphens.
- No passive voice.
- No exclamation marks.
- No emoji.
- Every paragraph scannable in 2 seconds on a phone screen.

### Hook Patterns (for reply drafts and post openings)

1. **The Reframe:** "[Thing everyone is doing]. Wrong [noun]."
2. **The Prediction:** "You're about to [painful thing] and [consequence]."
3. **The Question:** "Where in your [system] does [critical thing] happen?"
4. **The Counter:** "[Common belief] is [wrong/backwards]."
5. **The Direct Address:** "If you're [doing X], [thing you need to know]."
